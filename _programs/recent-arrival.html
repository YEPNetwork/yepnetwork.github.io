Newcomer

A newcomer is an individual that has migrated to the United States.  

  • Individuals of Ethiopian descent who have recently arrived to the United States of America

  • Individuals who are just starting a new life in the United States America

  • Individuals who have moved to the United States America with the intent to work or reside for more than one year.

  • Individuals who had limited or no access to the United States of America social, economic and professional lifestyle

  • Individuals who recently left Ethiopia and are looking to settle in the United States of America

  • Individuals who are new to the Washington D.C. tristate area.

  • Individual must have been in the United States of America less than three to five years.

Vision

Your Ethiopian Professional’s Newcomer Program focuses on helping those we serve learn and thrive in their new communities.

Mission

This Newcomer Program initiative attempts to offer sustainable resettlement programs and mobilize community leaders to offer these professional services that are necessary for our new residents. 

Newcomer Program Goals

The Newcomer Program aims to work towards the following goals:

  • To serve as a resource and work in three vital areas of need.

    • Educational Assistance Program

    • Cultural Programs

    • Community Partnerships

  • To create partnerships and working relationships with community organizations that offer services in education, cultural and legal affairs.

  • To ensure that program resources integrate with community needs.

Educational Program

  • Goal: to serve as a resource and provide a platform for learning.

  • Action:

    • Provide educational resource guides

    • Partner with educational firms to provide necessary classes.

  • Inputs: Resource content, printing needs, ESL tutors, and performance space

  • Activities:

    • Pass out resource guides (kit) at Empower Event,

    • Weekly/ monthly courses, TBD

      • Course Example: - English as a Second Language

  • Outputs: 200+ individuals served annually

  • Outcomes:

    • Long Term

      • Increase YEP’s program exposure and value in the community.

      • Increase educational knowledge, benefits, and accessibility in the community.

      • Encourage sense of community and social discourse.

    • Short Term

      • Newcomer individuals get information on the educational resources they need.

      • Newcomer Program impact and exposure.

      • Newcomer Program gets more referrals.

  • Cultural Program

    • Goal: to provide a platform for newcomer individuals (or group) to get access to information and perform activities that will make their acculturation process more successful.

    • Action: create cultural activities, creative group outings and panels

    • Inputs: volunteers, performance space, video editing, developing content, printing needs, and communication needs

    • Activities:

      • Monthly Group Activities - Host events, like Happy hours, cultural tours, museums strolls, etc

      • Quarterly Panels – discussing cultural nuances and hot topics

        • Topic Example: - “The Importance of Body Language”, “importance of Eye Contact”

    • Outputs: 200+ newcomer individuals served annually

    • Outcomes:

      • Long Term

        • Increase YEP’s program exposure and value in the community.

        • Increase knowledge in the cultural nuances that exist between the two cultures.

        • Encourage sense of community and social discourse.

      • Short Term

        • Newcomer individuals understand the American cultural expectations.

        • Newcomer Program impact and exposure.

        • Newcomer Program gets more referrals

  • Community Partnerships - Legal

    • Goal: to provide a safe space for newcomer individuals (or group) to get legal support and guidance.

    • Action: partner with local legal firms to provide, facilitate and structure legal support.

    • Inputs: legal firms, immigration lawyers, resource content, printing needs and performance space

    • Activities:

      • Pass out legal resource guides at events, and have available online.

      • Quarterly legal sessions (one on one, panel discussion, public forums) covering FYI topics.

        • Topic Example: - “What are your rights as a H1B Visa holder?”

    • Outputs: 100 newcomer individuals served annually.

    • Outcomes:

      • Long Term

        • Increase YEP’s program exposure and value in the community.

        • Increase legal knowledge and accessibility in the community.

        • Encourage sense of community and social discourse.

      • Short Term

        • Newcomer individuals get the legal assistance they need.

        • Newcomer Program impact and exposure.

        • Newcomer Program gets more referrals.
